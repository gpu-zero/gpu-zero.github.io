Getting to Bengaluru

A quick guide for friends and family flying in for November 10–12, 2026. Plan ahead — flights are long and visas take time. Tell us where you're setting off from and the routes, times, and money tips below will tailor to your trip.

Travelling from?

Start early. Sort your visa and flights well ahead, and leave a day to shake off the jet lag.

✈️ Flights

  • Fly into Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) in Devanahalli, about 40 km / 1–2 hours north of the city center (traffic-dependent).
  • From California, there's no short way — plan on ~22–26 hours door to door. Most routings connect once through a Gulf hub (Emirates via Dubai, Qatar via Doha, Etihad via Abu Dhabi) or a European hub (Frankfurt, Paris, London, Amsterdam). San Francisco also has a nonstop to Bengaluru on Air India worth pricing out.
  • From New York, plan on ~18–22 hours door to door. The smoothest routes connect once through a Gulf hub (Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi); you can also fly Air India nonstop to Delhi or Mumbai and connect onward to BLR, or route through a European hub.
  • From Edinburgh, you're closest — roughly 13–16 hours with one stop. Connect through London (Heathrow) onto a nonstop to BLR, or take a Gulf hub — Doha (Qatar) and Dubai (Emirates) both fly direct from Edinburgh — before the final leg.
  • Book early for the November 10–12, 2026 window — fares climb as the date approaches.
  • Pre-arrange an airport pickup through your hotel, or use the Uber/Ola pickup zone. Keep your hotel address handy in writing.

🕐 Time difference

Bengaluru is 13½ hours ahead of California (Pacific Standard Time in November). When it's 9:00 AM in Los Angeles, it's 10:30 PM in Bengaluru — give yourself a couple of days to reset.

Bengaluru is 10½ hours ahead of New York (Eastern Standard Time in November). When it's 9:00 AM in New York, it's 7:30 PM in Bengaluru.

Bengaluru is 5½ hours ahead of the UK (GMT in November). When it's 9:00 AM in Edinburgh, it's 2:30 PM in Bengaluru.

💳 Money

  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹ / INR). Check the rate before you go — it hovers around 83–85 INR to $1.
  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹ / INR). Check the rate before you go — it hovers around 83–85 INR to $1.
  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹ / INR). Check the rate before you go — it hovers around 105–110 INR to £1.
  • Cards work at hotels, malls, and restaurants. Carry cash for autos, small shops, markets, and tips. ATMs are everywhere — withdrawing rupees on arrival usually beats currency-exchange counters.
  • Tell your bank and card issuer you're traveling so they don't flag your charges.
  • Locals pay with UPI apps, which are hard for short-term visitors to set up — cash plus a credit card covers you fine.

📱 Phone & Internet

  • Easiest option: an eSIM (e.g. Airalo) you activate before landing — data works the moment you arrive.
  • A local SIM (Airtel or Jio) is cheap but needs ID/passport paperwork and can take a little time to activate. Counters are available at the airport.
  • WhatsApp is the default for messaging and calls in India; download it before you go.

🚗 Getting Around

  • We strongly recommend Uber & Ola (Ola is the local rideshare app) — it's by far the easiest and safest way to get around. Book in-app so the fare and route are set, and your hotel address is handy for the driver.
  • Please don't plan to drive yourself. Bengaluru traffic is intense and fast-moving, road rules differ from home, and an international permit plus a rental is far more hassle than it's worth — a rideshare or a hired car-and-driver is cheaper and far less stressful.
  • Auto-rickshaws (the yellow-and-green three-wheelers) are great for short hops; ask for the meter or agree the fare first.
  • Namma Metro is clean and quick on its routes (Purple & Green lines, still expanding) — handy for avoiding traffic.
  • Rapido bike taxis are cheap and nimble in gridlock if you're comfortable on two wheels.
  • Traffic is heavy — budget extra time for anything across town, especially during rush hours.

🌤️ Weather & Packing

  • Bengaluru sits at ~900 m (3,000 ft) and has one of India's mildest climates — comfortable most of the year, cooler in the evenings.
  • In mid-November, expect warm, sunny days (around 27 °C / 81 °F) and cool evenings (around 17 °C / 63 °F). The heavy monsoon is over, though a brief shower is still possible — pack a light layer for evenings and a compact umbrella just in case.
  • Power is 230V, plug types C / D / M. Coming from the US, bring a plug adapter — and check your devices are dual-voltage (most phone and laptop chargers are; hair tools often aren't).
