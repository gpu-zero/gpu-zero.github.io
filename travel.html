Getting to Bengaluru

A quick guide for friends and family traveling from the US and the UK (hello, Edinburgh!). Plan ahead — flights are long and visas take time. When in doubt, give yourself a few extra days to settle in (and beat the jet lag).

Start early. Sort your visa and book flights well in advance. India runs 9.5 / 12.5 / 13.5 hours ahead of US time zones and about 4.5 / 5.5 hours ahead of the UK (GMT / BST), so build in a day to recover.

🛂 Visas

  • US and UK citizens need a visa to enter India. Most wedding guests use the e-Tourist Visa, applied for online before you fly.
  • Apply only through the official Government of India portal (indianvisaonline.gov.in). Many look-alike sites charge extra fees — avoid them.
  • Apply at least a couple of weeks ahead. You'll need a passport valid 6+ months beyond travel with blank pages, plus a digital photo and a passport scan.
  • Tourist e-Visas come in different durations (e.g. 30-day, 1-year, 5-year). Pick what fits your trip.

Visa rules and fees change — always confirm current requirements on the official site before applying.

✈️ Flights

  • Fly into Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) in Devanahalli, about 40 km / 1–2 hours north of the city center (traffic-dependent).
  • There are limited nonstops from the US; most routes connect once through a hub: Gulf carriers (Emirates via Dubai, Qatar via Doha, Etihad via Abu Dhabi) or European hubs (via Frankfurt, Paris, London, Amsterdam). Air India and US carriers also serve the route.
  • From Edinburgh / the UK, connect through London or a Gulf hub — Doha and Dubai both have direct flights from Edinburgh — before the final leg into BLR.
  • Total travel time runs roughly 18–24 hours door to hub to BLR. Book early for the [WEDDING DATE] window.
  • Pre-arrange an airport pickup through your hotel, or use the Uber/Ola pickup zone. Keep your hotel address handy in writing.

🏨 Where to stay

  • We've listed hotels and neighborhoods at every budget — with notes on which areas sit closest to the venue — on the Where to Stay page.
  • Because traffic is heavy, where you stay matters more than the distance on a map. Book early — rooms near the venue go fast in wedding season.

💳 Money

  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹ / INR). Check the current exchange rate before you go (it hovers around 80–85 INR to 1 USD, or roughly 105–110 INR to £1).
  • Cards work at hotels, malls, and restaurants. Carry cash for autos, small shops, markets, and tips. ATMs are everywhere — withdrawing rupees on arrival usually beats currency-exchange counters.
  • Tell your bank and card issuer you're traveling so they don't flag your charges.
  • Locals pay with UPI apps, which are hard for short-term visitors to set up — cash plus a credit card covers you fine.

📱 Phone & Internet

  • Easiest option: an eSIM (e.g. Airalo) you activate before landing — data works the moment you arrive.
  • A local SIM (Airtel or Jio) is cheap but needs ID/passport paperwork and can take a little time to activate. Counters are available at the airport.
  • Check whether your home carrier offers an international day pass — sometimes simplest for a short trip.
  • WhatsApp is the default for messaging and calls in India; download it before you go.

🚗 Getting Around

  • Uber & Ola (Ola is the local rival) are the easiest way to get around — book in-app so the fare and route are set.
  • Auto-rickshaws (the yellow-and-green three-wheelers) are great for short hops; ask for the meter or agree the fare first.
  • Namma Metro is clean and quick on its routes (Purple & Green lines, still expanding) — handy for avoiding traffic.
  • Rapido bike taxis are cheap and nimble in gridlock if you're comfortable on two wheels.
  • Bengaluru traffic is heavy — budget extra time for anything across town, especially during rush hours.
